1. Introduction and Scope

This Privacy Policy applies to all personal data collected through the iQdrone Academy website ([yourdomain.com]), mobile applications, enrollment forms, consultation bookings, and any other digital services we provide. By using our website or services, you consent to the data practices described in this policy [citation:1].

If you do not agree with this policy, please do not use our website or provide us with your personal information.

2. Information We Collect

We collect information to provide better services to our users. The categories of data we collect include:

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Identity Data: Full name, date of birth, gender, photograph (for ID cards).
  • Contact Data: Email address, phone number, postal address.
  • Educational Data: Qualifications, enrollment details, course progress, certifications.
  • Financial Data: Payment details, bank account information (processed securely through payment gateways – we do not store card details).
  • Communication Data: Messages you send via contact forms, emails, or consultation bookings.

2.2 Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our website, we automatically collect [citation:1][citation:3]:

  • Technical Data: IP address, browser type and version, device information, operating system.
  • Usage Data: Pages visited, time spent, click patterns, referral sources.
  • Cookie Data: We use cookies and similar tracking technologies (see Section 7 for details).
Note: Under the DPDPA, even automatically collected data like IP addresses qualifies as "personal data" if it can be linked to an individual [citation:2].

3. How We Use Your Information

We process your personal data for the following purposes [citation:1][citation:8]:

  • Service Delivery: To process enrollments, manage course access, issue certificates, and provide training.
  • Communication: To respond to inquiries, send course updates, and schedule consultations.
  • Improvement: To analyze website usage, enhance user experience, and develop new courses.
  • Legal Compliance: To comply with DGCA regulations, maintain records, and fulfill statutory obligations.
  • Marketing (with consent): To send promotional materials about new courses or workshops (you may opt out anytime).

4. Legal Basis for Processing

We process your personal data only when we have a valid legal basis, including [citation:1][citation:8]:

  • Consent: You have given clear consent for us to process your data for specific purposes.
  • Contract: Processing is necessary for the performance of a contract (e.g., course enrollment).
  • Legal Obligation: Processing is required to comply with Indian laws (e.g., DGCA training records).
  • Legitimate Interests: Processing is necessary for our legitimate interests (e.g., improving website functionality) that do not override your privacy rights.

5. Data Sharing and Disclosure

We respect your privacy and do not sell, rent, or trade your personal information. We may share data only in the following circumstances [citation:1][citation:3]:

  • Service Providers: With trusted third-party vendors who assist us in operating our website, processing payments, or delivering courses (e.g., payment gateways, email services). These providers are contractually bound to protect your data.
  • Legal Requirements: When required by law, court order, or government agency (e.g., DGCA, law enforcement).
  • Business Transfers: In the event of a merger, acquisition, or sale of assets, your data may be transferred with notice to you.
  • With Your Consent: In any other circumstances with your explicit consent.

6. Data Security

We implement reasonable security practices and procedures to protect your personal data from unauthorized access, loss, or misuse [citation:1][citation:10]:

  • SSL encryption for all data transmitted through our website.
  • Secure servers and firewalls.
  • Access controls limiting data access to authorized personnel only.
  • Regular security audits and monitoring.

However, no method of transmission over the Internet is 100% secure. While we strive to protect your data, we cannot guarantee absolute security [citation:3].

7. Cookie Policy

Our website uses cookies to enhance your browsing experience. Cookies are small text files stored on your device [citation:1].

Types of cookies we use:

  • Essential Cookies: Required for website functionality (e.g., session management).
  • Analytics Cookies: To understand how visitors interact with our site (e.g., Google Analytics).
  • Preference Cookies: To remember your settings and preferences.

When you first visit our site, you will see a cookie consent banner asking for your explicit consent before non-essential cookies are set. You can manage cookie preferences through your browser settings [citation:2].

8. Data Retention

We retain your personal data only as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including legal, accounting, or reporting requirements [citation:1][citation:8].

  • Student Records: Retained for 5 years after course completion as required by DGCA regulations.
  • Inquiry Data: Retained for 2 years after last contact.
  • Website Logs: IP addresses and usage data retained for 6 months for security analysis.

After the retention period, data is securely deleted or anonymized.

9. Your Rights Under DPDPA

Under the Digital Personal Data Protection Act, 2023, you have the following rights regarding your personal data [citation:2][citation:8]:

  • Right to Access: Request a summary of personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Correction: Update or correct inaccurate data.
  • Right to Erasure: Request deletion of your data (subject to legal retention requirements).
  • Right to Withdraw Consent: Withdraw consent for processing at any time.
  • Right to Nominate: Nominate a representative to exercise rights in case of death or incapacity.
  • Right to Grievance Redressal: Lodge complaints about data processing.

To exercise these rights, please contact our Grievance Officer using the details in Section 13.

10. Children's Privacy

Our courses and workshops are designed for students of all ages, including minors (under 18 years). Under the DPDPA, we require verifiable parental consent before collecting personal data from children [citation:2][citation:9].

For students under 18, a parent or guardian must complete the enrollment form and provide consent. We do not engage in tracking or targeted advertising aimed at children.

If you believe we have inadvertently collected data from a child without parental consent, please contact us immediately.
